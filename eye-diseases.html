Eye Diseases & Conditions

Early detection saves vision. Learn about the most common eye conditions, their symptoms, and how our specialists can help.

2.2 Billion people affected by vision impairment globally
Common

Myopia (Nearsightedness)

A condition where close objects appear clearly but distant objects are blurry. One of the most prevalent eye conditions worldwide.

Key Symptoms

  • Blurry distant vision
  • Squinting to see far
  • Eye strain & headaches
Book Consultation
Serious
Early Detection Critical

Glaucoma

Often called the "silent thief of sight" — glaucoma damages the optic nerve gradually, usually with no pain or early symptoms.

Key Symptoms

  • Gradual peripheral vision loss
  • Eye pain & redness
  • Halos around lights
Book Consultation
Age-Related

Cataracts

A clouding of the eye's natural lens, leading to blurred vision. Cataracts are the leading cause of blindness worldwide but are highly treatable.

Key Symptoms

  • Cloudy or foggy vision
  • Faded colors
  • Poor night vision
Book Consultation
Serious

Diabetic Retinopathy

A diabetes complication that affects the eyes by damaging blood vessels in the retina. Can lead to blindness if untreated.

Key Symptoms

  • Spots or floaters
  • Blurred vision
  • Dark areas in vision
Book Consultation
Common

Conjunctivitis (Pink Eye)

Inflammation of the conjunctiva — the thin clear tissue that lines the eyelid. Usually caused by infection or allergies.

Key Symptoms

  • Red or pink eyes
  • Discharge & crusting
  • Itching & irritation
Book Consultation
In Children

Amblyopia (Lazy Eye)

A condition where vision in one eye doesn't develop properly during childhood. Early treatment is essential for best outcomes.

Key Symptoms

  • One eye wandering
  • Poor depth perception
  • Squinting or closing one eye
Book Consultation
Age-Related

Macular Degeneration

Age-related macular degeneration (AMD) affects the central part of the retina (macula), causing loss of central vision.

Key Symptoms

  • Blurry central vision
  • Distorted straight lines
  • Dark spot in center of vision
Book Consultation
In Children

Strabismus (Crossed Eyes)

A condition where the eyes don't look in the same direction at the same time. Common in children and highly treatable.

Key Symptoms

  • Eyes pointing in different directions
  • Double vision
  • Tilting head to see clearly
Book Consultation
Common

Dry Eye Syndrome

Occurs when tears don't adequately lubricate the eyes. Increasingly common due to screen time and environmental factors.

Key Symptoms

  • Stinging or burning eyes
  • Sensitivity to light
  • Blurred vision
